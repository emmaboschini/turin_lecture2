The file **test 1.txt** provides a comprehensive overview of **Development Cooperation**, focusing on its evolution, criteria, and significance in the global landscape.

## Key Themes

*   **Definitions & Evolution**: Explains the shift from "international development" (focused on poorer nations) to "global development" (emphasizing global interdependence, public goods, and the climate crisis).
*   **Motivations**: Actors engage in development assistance for altruistic, economic, political, or humanitarian reasons.
*   **Global Frameworks**: Highlights the importance of the UN’s **2030 Agenda** and the **Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs)**, and how global events like the COVID-19 pandemic have exposed structural vulnerabilities.
*   **Criteria for Cooperation**: To be defined as "development cooperation," activities must:
    *   Explicitly support development priorities.
    *   Be non-profit (though they may encourage positive economic outcomes).
    *   Intentionally favor developing countries.
    *   Be based on cooperative relationships and ownership.
*   **Italian Context**: Mentions Italian laws (e.g., Law 125 of 2014) that integrate development cooperation into foreign policy to promote peace, human rights, and poverty reduction.
*   **Distinction from Aid**: Clarifies that development cooperation is broader than simple "aid," encompassing policy changes, capacity building, and international partnerships.

## Conclusion
Development cooperation is a complex field that combines political, economic, and social dimensions to improve living conditions worldwide through non-profit-oriented actions and sustainable partnerships.
